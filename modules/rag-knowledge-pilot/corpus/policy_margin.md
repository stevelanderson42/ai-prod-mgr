# Margin Requirements and Restrictions

## Initial Margin

All customer margin accounts must meet a minimum initial deposit of $2,000 or 100% of the purchase price, whichever is less, before any margin trading activity is permitted. The firm reserves the right to impose higher initial margin requirements for concentrated positions or volatile securities.

## Maintenance Margin

- Equity in a margin account must not fall below 25% of the current market value of the securities held.
- If equity drops below the maintenance threshold, a margin call will be issued requiring the customer to deposit additional funds or liquidate positions within three business days.
- The firm may liquidate positions without prior notice if the account equity falls below 20% during periods of extreme market volatility.

## Restricted Securities

Certain securities are ineligible for margin lending, including penny stocks (securities trading below $5 per share on OTC markets), initial public offerings within the first 30 days of trading, and any security designated as non-marginable by the firm's credit risk department. Registered representatives must verify margin eligibility before accepting any margin order.
