# Communications Standards — Advertising, Correspondence, and Recordkeeping

## Fair and Balanced Requirement

All communications with the public — including retail communications, correspondence, and institutional communications — must be fair, balanced, and not misleading. Every statement of benefit must be accompanied by a clear disclosure of the associated risks and limitations.

## Prohibited Content

- **Promissory statements:** Communications must not predict or project future performance, guarantee returns, or imply that past performance will be repeated.
- **Exaggerated claims:** Superlatives such as "best," "safest," or "guaranteed" are prohibited unless the claim can be substantiated and is approved by compliance.
- **Omission of material facts:** Omitting material risks or costs that would be necessary for a customer to make an informed decision is a violation.

## Pre-Use Review and Approval

Retail communications (materials distributed to more than 25 retail investors within a 30-day period) must be submitted to the compliance department for pre-use review and approval. Registered representatives may not distribute unapproved retail communications.

## Recordkeeping

All communications — including emails, social media posts, text messages, and printed materials — must be retained for a minimum of three years from the date of last use or distribution. The firm must be able to produce any retained communication within 48 hours of a regulatory request.
