# Suitability Obligations and Customer Profiling

## Know Your Customer

Before recommending any investment product, the registered representative must collect and document the customer's financial profile, including:

- Annual income and liquid net worth
- Investment time horizon (short-term, intermediate, long-term)
- Risk tolerance (conservative, moderate, aggressive, speculative)
- Investment objectives (capital preservation, income, growth, speculation)
- Tax status and any legal or regulatory constraints on the account

## Reasonable-Basis and Customer-Specific Suitability

The firm enforces a two-part suitability standard. First, a registered representative must have a reasonable basis to believe the product is suitable for at least some investors (reasonable-basis suitability). Second, the representative must have a reasonable basis to believe the specific recommendation is suitable for the particular customer based on their documented profile (customer-specific suitability).

## Heightened Suitability for Complex Products

Structured products, leveraged ETFs, variable annuities, and alternative investments require enhanced suitability review. The representative must complete a product-specific training module and obtain supervisory pre-approval before recommending any complex product. The customer must acknowledge in writing that they understand the material risks of the product.
